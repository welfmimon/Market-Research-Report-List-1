<p><p>The 6-inch Indium Phosphide (InP) wafer market is categorized into N-type and P-type segments based on doping characteristics. N-type wafers have extra electrons, enhancing electrical conductivity, making them suitable for high-speed electronics and optoelectronic applications. Conversely, P-type wafers have a deficiency of electrons, allowing for hole conduction, which is vital in creating p-n junctions essential for diodes and transistors. Both types are crucial in developing advanced semiconductor devices, including lasers and photodetectors, catering to growing technological demands.</p></p>
